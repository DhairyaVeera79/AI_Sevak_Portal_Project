# AI Sevak Portal — Presentation Narrative

## 1) Problem We Are Solving

SRLC’s seva operations are growing, but volunteer coordination is still fragmented across manual processes and informal channels.

### Current Pain Points
- Volunteer onboarding and seva joining are often inconsistent and partially off-paper.
- Communication is spread across disconnected groups, causing missed updates and repeated instructions.
- Seva leaders spend significant time on repetitive onboarding and follow-up.
- Effort tracking, reporting, and impact storytelling are hard due to unstructured data.
- As volunteer volume increases, process quality and governance become difficult to maintain.

### Why It Matters Now
- The mission is scaling across more projects and contributors.
- Without process standardization, leadership bandwidth gets consumed by coordination overhead.
- A structured platform is needed to preserve quality while scaling service delivery.

---

## 2) Proposed Solution

The AI Sevak Portal is a unified internal platform for the full volunteer lifecycle.

### Core Product Modules
- Registration and profile setup (skills, availability, location, interests).
- Seva discovery and AI-assisted allocation guidance.
- Structured onboarding (general + seva-specific + progress briefings).
- Calendar and hours logging.
- Seva-scoped in-app messaging.
- Daily/weekly logs with text, image, and video.
- Authorized expense logging with document attachments.
- Role-based dashboards with approvals and moderation.

### AI’s Role (Assistive, Not Autonomous)
- Recommends suitable sevas using profile and availability data.
- Answers onboarding questions from approved internal content.
- Summarizes ongoing seva progress for joiners and leaders.
- Drafts periodic timeline summaries for human review.

### Expected Operational Outcomes
- Faster volunteer-to-seva matching.
- Reduced repetitive manual onboarding.
- Better visibility for leaders and trustees.
- Stronger data backbone for impact communication.

### Donation-to-Impact Storytelling Layer
- Moderated logs data (text/image/video) is converted into structured impact timelines.
- Each approved story links contribution inputs to seva activities and outcomes.
- Leadership-approved story cards and monthly digests show where support is going and what is changing on ground.
- This enables transparent, dignified communication for both beneficiaries and supporters.

## 3) Who Inside SRLC Uses and Benefits

### Primary User Groups
- C4 Sevaks (Volunteers): clearer joining process, fewer coordination gaps, better visibility of responsibilities.
- C3 Seva Coordinators/Managers: smoother day-to-day tracking, follow-up, and communication.
- C2 Seva Leaders: reduced admin load, improved approvals and team readiness.
- C1 Trustees/Leadership: portfolio-level visibility across seva operations and outcomes.
- Platform/Admin Team: governance controls, moderation workflows, and system oversight.

### Beneficiaries by Value Type
- **Operational efficiency:** C2/C3 teams spend less time on repetitive tasks.
- **Volunteer experience:** C4 users get a cleaner, structured journey.
- **Decision quality:** C1/C2 get more reliable and timely operational data.
- **Institutional memory:** all teams benefit from timeline archives and searchable history.
